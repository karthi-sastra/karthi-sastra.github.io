About

Professor [Your Name] leads an interdisciplinary research program at the intersection of computational chemistry, structural biology, and medicinal chemistry. With over 15 years of experience in computer-aided drug design (CADD), our lab focuses on developing novel therapeutic strategies for cardiovascular diseases and emerging infectious diseases.

Our research integrates structure-based drug design, molecular dynamics simulations, QSAR modeling, and AI/ML approaches to accelerate drug discovery pipelines. We maintain strong collaborations with experimental groups to validate computational predictions and advance promising candidates toward clinical development.

Research Interests

  • Structure-based virtual screening and molecular docking
  • Free energy calculations (MM/PBSA, MM/GBSA)
  • QSAR and machine learning in drug discovery
  • Targeting protein-protein interactions
  • Multi-target drug design for complex diseases
  • Immunoinformatics and vaccine design
  • AI-driven de novo drug design

Research

Cardiovascular Drug Discovery

Development of novel small molecule inhibitors targeting key enzymes in hypertension and atherosclerosis pathways. Our recent work focuses on allosteric modulators of ACE2 and optimization of dual-acting cardiovascular agents using structure-based approaches.

Key Methods: Molecular dynamics, Free energy perturbation, Pharmacophore modeling

Antiviral Therapeutics

Design of broad-spectrum antiviral agents targeting conserved regions of viral proteases and polymerases. Current projects include inhibitors of SARS-CoV-2 main protease (Mpro) and RNA-dependent RNA polymerase (RdRp) with improved resistance profiles.

Key Methods: Structure-based virtual screening, MM/PBSA calculations, ADMET prediction

AI-Enhanced Drug Design

Implementation of deep learning architectures for de novo molecular generation and property prediction. Our lab develops specialized neural networks for scaffold hopping and multi-parameter optimization in lead compound development.

Key Methods: Graph neural networks, Reinforcement learning, Generative models

Immunoinformatics

Computational epitope prediction and vaccine design for emerging pathogens. We employ immunoinformatics pipelines to identify conserved T-cell and B-cell epitopes with broad population coverage for next-generation vaccine candidates.

Key Methods: MHC binding prediction, Epitope conservancy analysis, Population coverage calculation

Teaching & Courses

PHA 601: Advanced Drug Design

Graduate-level course covering modern computational approaches in drug discovery, including molecular docking, pharmacophore modeling, and structure-based design principles.

Topics: Molecular recognition, Binding free energy calculations, Fragment-based drug design

PHA 545: Medicinal Chemistry

Comprehensive overview of drug-target interactions, structure-activity relationships, and lead optimization strategies with emphasis on cardiovascular and antiviral agents.

Topics: QSAR, Prodrug design, Multi-target directed ligands

PHA 712: Drug Informatics

Advanced course on cheminformatics, bioinformatics, and AI applications in pharmaceutical sciences, including hands-on training with industry-standard software.

Topics: Molecular descriptors, Machine learning, High-throughput screening data analysis

PHA 625: Immunoinformatics

Specialized course focusing on computational immunology methods for vaccine design, including epitope prediction, immunogenicity assessment, and reverse vaccinology.

Topics: MHC polymorphism, Epitope conservancy, Vaccine adjuvant design
